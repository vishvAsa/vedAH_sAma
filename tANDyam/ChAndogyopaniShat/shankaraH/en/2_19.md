***Upaniṣad text:***

**The Hair is the syllable ‘*Hiṃ*’; Wie Skin is the *Prastāva*; the Flesh is the *Udgītha*; the Bone is the Pratihāra; the Marrow is the *Nidhana*.—This is the *Yajñāyajñīya (Sāma), interwoven in the parts of the Body*.—(1)**

**Commentary (Śaṅkara Bhāṣya):**

*The Hair is the syllable ‘Hiṃ*’,—it being the first of the parts of the Body.—*The Skin is the Prastāva*,—becaṇse it is next (to the Hair).—*The Flesh is the Udgītha*; because of its importance.—*The Bone is the Pratihāra*,— because it is concentrated.—*The Marrow is the Nidhana*;— because it is the last.—This is the Sāma named “*Yajñāyajñīya*”,—interwoven in the parts of the Body.—(1)

***Upaniṣad text:***

**One who thus knows the ‘*Yajñāyajñīya*’ as interwoven in the parts of the Body becomes equipped with limbs, is not crippled in his limbs; he attains the full life-span, lives gloriously, and becomes great in offspring and cattle, great in fame. His observance is that he should not eat marrows for a year,—or that he should not eat marrows at all.—(2)**

**Commentary (Śaṅkara Bhāṣya):**

Becomes equipped with limbs:—that is, he has all his limbs whole—Is not crippled in his limbs;—*i.e*., none of his limbs—Hands, feet and the rest,—is crooked: that is, he does not become lame or crippled in his hands.—*For a year*—just for a year only—he should not eat marrow, i.e., meat. The plural form (‘marrows’) is meant to include *fish* also. Or, his observance is that he should not ever, at all times,—*eat marrows at all*—(2)

End of Section (19) of Discourse II
