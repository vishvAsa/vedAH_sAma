31. He may even use the flesh of animals killed by carnivorous beasts. [^18] 


[^18]:  'They declare, that baiá¹£ka means the flesh of an animal, slain by a tiger or the like. He may use even that. The word "even" implies blame. Hence this is a rule for times of distress, and it must be understood that such food is to be eaten only on failure of roots and fruits and the like.'--Haradatta. The commentator adds that the flesh of forbidden animals must be avoided.
