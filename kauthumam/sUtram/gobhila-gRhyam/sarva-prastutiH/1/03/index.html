मूलम्

अग्निमुपसमाधाय परिसमूह्य दक्षिणजान्वक्तो दक्षिणेनाग्निमदितेऽनुमन्य-स्वेत्युदकाञ्जलिं प्रसिञ्चेत् १

Oldenberg
  1. 1 Having put wood on the (sacred) fire, having swept"(the ground) round it, he should, bending his right knee, pour out to the south of the fire his joined hands full of water with (the words), ‘Aditi! Give thy consent!’

  1. To the west with (the words), ‘Anumati! Give thy consent!’

देव सवितः प्रसुवेति प्रदक्षिणम् अग्निं पर्युक्षेत् सकृद्वा त्रिर् वा ४

मूलम्

देव सवितः प्रसुवेति प्रदक्षिणमग्निं पर्युक्षेत्सकृद्वा त्रिर्वा ४

Oldenberg
  1. With (the words), ‘God Savitṛ! Give thy impulse!’ (Mantra-Brāhmaṇa I, 1, 1) he should sprinkle (water) round the fire once or thrice so as to keep his right side turned towards it

पर्युक्षणान्तान् व्यतिहरन्न्, अभिपर्युक्षन् होमीयम् +++(अन्नादिकम्)+++ ५

सर्वाष् टीकाः ...{Loading}...
मूलम्

पर्युक्षणान्तान्व्यतिहरन्नभिपर्युक्षन्होमीयम् ५

Oldenberg
  1. Interchanging the points at which he begins and ends the (sprinkling of water), and sprinkling so as to encompass what he is going to offer (with the streams of water).

अथ हविष्यस्यान्नस्याग्नौ जुहुयात् कृतस्य वाकृतस्य वा ६

  1. 2 Let him then make oblations of the sacrificial food, be it prepared or raw, over the fire.

मूलम्

अकृतं चेत्प्रक्षाल्य जुहुयात्प्रोदकं कृत्वा ७

Oldenberg
  1. 3 If it is raw, he should sacrifice after having washed it and having let the water drop off.

08 अथ यदि दधि ...{Loading}...

अथ यदि दधि पयो यवागूं वा कँसेन वा चरुस्थाल्या वा स्रुवेण वैव ८

सर्वाष् टीकाः ...{Loading}...
मूलम्

अथ यदि दधि पयो यवागूं वा कँ सेन वा चरुस्थाल्या वा स्रुवेण वैव ८

Oldenberg
  1. If it consists in curds or milk or rice gruel, (he should sacrifice it) with a brazen bowl, or with the pot in which the oblations of boiled rice are prepared, or also with the (sacrificial spoon called) Sruva;

मूलम्

अग्नये स्वाहेति पूर्वां तूष्णीमुत्तरां मध्ये चापराजितायां च दिशीति सायम् ९

Oldenberg
  1. 4 In the evening the first (oblation) with (the formula), ‘To Agni Svāhā!’ the second silently, in the middle and in the north-eastern part (of the fire);

10 अथ प्रातः सूर्याय ...{Loading}...

अथ प्रातः सूर्याय स्वाहेति पूर्वां, तूष्णीम् एवोत्तरां - मध्ये चैवापराजितायां+++(=ऐशान्यायां)+++ चैव दिशि १०

सर्वाष् टीकाः ...{Loading}...
मूलम्

अथ प्रातः सूर्याय स्वाहेति पूर्वां तूष्णीमेवोत्तरां मध्ये चैवापराजितायां चैव दिशि १०

Oldenberg
  1. In the morning the first (oblation) with (the formula), ‘To Sūrya Svāhā!’ the second again silently, again in the middle and in the north-eastern part (of the fire).

11 समिधमाधायानुपर्युक्ष्य तथैवोदकाञ्जलीन्प्रसिञ्चेदन्वमँ स्था ...{Loading}...

सर्वाष् टीकाः ...{Loading}...
मूलम्

समिधमाधायानुपर्युक्ष्य तथैवोदकाञ्जलीन्प्रसिञ्चेदन्वमँ स्था इति मन्त्रविशेषः ११

Oldenberg
  1. Having put a piece of wood (on the fire), and having again sprinkled (water) round it, he should pour out again his joined hands full of water in the same way (as prescribed in the Sūtras 1-3); in the Mantras he says, ‘Thou hast given thy consent’ (instead of ‘Give thy consent’).

मूलम्

प्रदक्षिणमग्निं परिक्रम्यापां शेषं निनीय पूरयित्वा चमसं प्रतिष्ठाप्य यथार्थम् १२

Oldenberg
  1. 5 Having circumambulated the fire so as to turn his right side towards it, having poured out the remains of water, and filled the vessel again, and put it (in its proper place), (he may do) whatever his business is.

  1. 1-5. Khādira-Gṛhya I, 2, 17-21. ↩︎

  2. The sacrificial food is either kṛta (prepared) or akṛta (unprepared). A mess of boiled rice, for instance, is kṛta, rice grains are akṛta. ↩︎

  3. 7-12. Khādira-Gṛhya I, 5, 10-12. Prodaka in Sūtra 7 is explained by pragatodaka. ↩︎

  4. 9-10. Khādira-Gṛhya, l.l. 13-15. ↩︎

  5. The water is that mentioned chap. 1, 24. With regard to p. 21 yathārtham the commentary says, ‘yathārthaṃ karmāpavargavihitaṃ Vāmadevyagānādikaṃ prātarāhutipaścādvihitaṃ brahmayajñaṃ vā kuryād iti vākyaśeṣaḥ.’ Similarly in the note on II, 4, 11 it is said, ‘yathārtham iti karmaṇaḥ parisamāptir ucyate;’ II, 8, 17: ‘yathārthaṃ tantrasamāpanaṃ kuryāt,’ &c. In my translation I have adopted the opinion of Professor Weber (Indische Studien, V, 375), according to whom yathārtham simply means, ‘(he should behave) as required by circumstances;’ ‘(he should do) what happens to be his business.’ ↩︎

  6. The last words are ā jīvitāvabhṛthāt, which literally means ’till the Avabhṛtha bath of his life.’ The Avabhṛtha bath is the bath taken at the end of certain sacrifices, so that the Avabhṛtha of life signifies death. ↩︎

  7. Khādira-Gṛhya I, 5, 17. ↩︎

  8. 16-18. Khādira-Gṛhya, l.l. 18, 19. In my translation of Sūtra 17 I have adopted, though not quite without doubt, the conjecture of Professor Roth given in Professor Knauer’s note, p. 137. Professor Roth writes ṛtebhaṅgayā vācā or ṛtebhagayā vācā: he says simply ‘om,’ and not ‘ó-o-ó-o-ó-om.’ According to the commentary Sūtra 17 would refer to the wife, not to the husband. ↩︎

  9. The MSS. give mākhyā and mākṣā. We ought to read, tan ma kṣāyīty upāṃśu. Comp. Āpastamba II, 2, 3, 11 ↩︎
