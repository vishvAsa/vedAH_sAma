सन्धिवेलयोरुपस्थानं स्वस्त्ययनमादित्य नावमित्युद्यन्तं त्वादित्यानूदियासमिति पूर्वाह्णे प्रतितिष्ठन्तं त्वादित्यानुप्रति-तिष्ठासमित्यपराह्णे १२

Oldenberg
  1. Worshipping (the sun) at the time of the morning twilight and of the evening twilight procures happiness, (both times) with (the formula), ‘O sun! the ship’ (MB. II, 5, 14), and (after that) in the morning with (the formula), ‘When thou risest, O sun, I shall rise with thee’ (ibid. 15); in the evening with (the formula), ‘When thou goest to rest, O sun, I shall go to rest with thee’ (ibid. 16).

  1. 7 At the evening twilight (of every day of that fortnight), having left the village in a westerly direction, and having put wood on the fire at a place where four roads meet, he should sacrifice the small grains (of that rice), turning his face towards the sun, with (the words), ‘To Bhala Svāhā!
