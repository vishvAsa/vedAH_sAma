<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

एवमत ऊर्ध्वं गृह्येऽग्नौ जुहुयाद्वा हावयेद्वाजीवितावभृथात् १३
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

13. [^8]  In that way, from that time (in which he has begun to offer the two daily sacrifices) he should sacrifice, or should have sacrificed, over the (sacred) domestic fire, till the end of his life.


[^8]:  The last words are ā jīvitāvabhṛthāt, which literally means 'till the Avabhṛtha bath of his life.' The Avabhṛtha bath is the bath taken at the end of certain sacrifices, so that the Avabhṛtha of life signifies death.
</details>
